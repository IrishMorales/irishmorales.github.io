Building systems that put people first

Philosophy: Why I build things

"Made with ❤️" should mean more than passion.
If you search "Made with ❤️", you'll get 30 million+ hits. It's a relic from early Web 2.0.

The early Web 2.0 internet was a sandbox, and everyone wanted to play.
Back then, tech meant nerds staying up late, eating pizza, coding weird little things for the hell of it. I was 14 then, learning C++ from 1990s library books. People made Flash games and funny Twitter bots. People were hopeful about tech.

Now, it's a strip mine with a paywall and ads hyper-optimized to Goodhart's Law.
You want to watch a video, but Youtube plays two ads longer than the video itself. You get pop-ups filling half the screen. You need a subscription to keep something you already own. Enshittification is the great heat death of the Internet.

Code doesn't live in Git repositories.
It lives on servers draining rivers, minerals mined by laborers, and upkeep costs. Code is born into the same systems as us, and when those fail, tech will too. Tech is not exempt from entropy.

We need tech that remembers it's for people.
The industry needs ways to fund innovation without bleeding users dry. Hopefully this is just the trough of the hype cycle, the local minimum of tech's gradient descent. Maybe we had to break things to learn how to build them right.

For that we need compassion. Passion builds things, but compassion builds things that matter.
“Made with ❤️” shouldn’t just mean I cared about making it.
It should mean I cared about you.
